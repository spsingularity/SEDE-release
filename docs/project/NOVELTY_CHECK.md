# Novelty check — is SEDE's core mechanism new?

**Question.** Has anyone previously proposed SEDE's distinctive claim: that **real dark energy is
*sourced* by cosmic structure formation, via the binding-energy entropy of collapsed structure being
deposited on the cosmic horizon** (ρ_DE = T_AH·s_grav, with s_grav gated by structure growth `f_sat`,
volume-law Δ=1)?

**Verdict (UPDATED after a broader pass, June 2026).** The first focused search missed it, but a
broader pass found a **clear precedent for the *core idea*** — *dark energy sourced by the entropy/
information of structure (star) formation* — in **Michael Gough's Holographic Dark Information Energy
(HDIE, 2011)** and his 2025 evidence paper "Dark Energy Driven by Star Formation." So the headline
"structure feeds dark energy" is **NOT new to SEDE.** What remains plausibly distinctive is SEDE's
**specific mechanism** (horizon thermodynamics / gravitational binding entropy on the apparent horizon /
Barrow volume-law Δ=1 / ρ_DE = T_AH·s_grav / parameter-free / w crossing −1), which differs from
Gough's Landauer-information-of-hot-baryons mechanism. **Bottom line: SEDE's novelty is much narrower
than first stated — it is a particular horizon-thermodynamic realization of an idea (structure-sourced
entropic dark energy) that Gough pioneered, not the originator of that idea.** A formal ADS/INSPIRE
priority pass is still required, now focused on whether SEDE's *specific* horizon-binding-entropy
mechanism is distinct from Gough's information version and from the entropic-cosmology family.

*(Methodological note: the first pass searched "horizon entropy / Clausius" phrasings and missed Gough,
who frames it as "information energy / star formation." This is exactly why the broader pass mattered —
and a caution that even this is not provably exhaustive.)*

## 0. THE CLOSEST PRECEDENT — Gough's Holographic Dark Information Energy (HDIE / IDE)
**M. P. Gough, "Holographic Dark Information Energy," *Entropy* 13 (2011) 924; and "Evidence for Dark
Energy Driven by Star Formation: Information Dark Energy?", *Entropy* (2025).** This is a direct
precedent for SEDE's *core claim*:
- **Same headline:** dark energy is the energy of the **entropy/information produced as structure (stars,
  heated gas/plasma) forms** — it *grows with star formation* until it accelerates the universe.
- **Same coincidence-problem solution + self-regulation:** acceleration suppresses star formation, which
  caps the information-energy density — "the era of acceleration will be clearly limited in time."
- **Same kind of test SEDE proposes (E1):** Gough's 2025 paper **fits the observed stellar-mass-density
  (structure) history to the dark-energy EOS** from Planck/Pantheon+/DES/DESI, using
  (H/H₀)² = Ω a⁻³ + Ω_IDE·(SMD(a)/SMD₀)^p, **best fit p = 0.5** (R² = 0.93). This is essentially a
  "structure-history drives dark energy" fit — the empirical heart of SEDE's mechanism claim.

**How SEDE differs from Gough (the genuinely distinctive part, if any):**
| | Gough HDIE/IDE | SEDE |
|---|---|---|
| entropy used | **information/thermal** entropy of star-heated gas & plasma (∝ T, Landauer k_BT ln2 per bit) | **gravitational/horizon** entropy (Barrow volume-law, Δ=1) |
| where it lives | information energy **in the bulk** (hot baryons) | entropy **on the apparent horizon** |
| mechanism | Landauer's principle + holographic bound | Clausius first law on the apparent horizon, ρ_DE = T_AH·s_grav |
| structure driver | stellar mass density SMD(a)^{0.5} | growth-factor gate f_sat(D), λ = 0.5 (Δ=1) |
| EOS | fitted CPL | crosses w = −1, parameter-free |
| QG superstructure | minimal (phenomenological) | full (Barrow / volume-law / dS-holography postulate) |

So SEDE and Gough **agree on the central physical idea** (structure's entropy *is* dark energy, self-
regulating, coincidence-solving, structure-history-driven) and **differ on the microphysical mechanism**
(gravitational horizon entropy vs information entropy of hot baryons). The intriguing coincidence that
both land on a **"0.5" exponent** (Gough's SMD power p=0.5; SEDE's λ=0.5 ⟺ Δ=1) is worth examining — it
may be a deep correspondence or a numerical accident.

**Honest consequence:** SEDE can **no longer claim to originate** "dark energy sourced by structure
formation." It can, at most, claim a **distinct mechanism** for a known idea — and even that must be
checked against the entropic-cosmology family below.

## The nearest neighbors and how each differs

### 1. Entropic-force / entropic dark energy (Easson–Frampton–Smoot lineage)
Acceleration from an entropic force **at the horizon itself**.
- **Difference (and it's telling):** the source is the horizon, *not* structure — and these models are
  known to **struggle with structure formation**. The literature explicitly states that *"the existence
  of growing modes for structure formation is incompatible with the accelerated expansion in standard
  entropic-force dark energy models."*
- **SEDE is the inverse:** it makes structure the *source* of dark energy and keeps growth healthy
  (smooth, c_s²=1 dark energy). SEDE turns this family's central *problem* into its *mechanism*.

### 2. Generalized-entropy / Barrow / Tsallis holographic dark energy
A modified **horizon entropy drives dark energy** (Cai–Kim gravity–thermodynamics); the large
2024–2026 "mass-to-horizon entropy" wave belongs here.
- **Difference — causal direction:** structure growth is treated as a *consequence/test* (how the
  Barrow/Tsallis exponent changes the growth rate), i.e. *entropy-form → growth*. SEDE reverses it:
  *growth → entropy → dark energy*. Same machinery, opposite arrow.
- SEDE's *background* is a special (Δ=1, parameter-fixed) member of Barrow holographic DE; the
  **structure-source term `f_sat` is the addition** that this family lacks.

### 3. Sivaram & Arun (2013), "Holography, Dark Energy and Entropy of Large Cosmic Structures"
Title looks like a bullseye, but on reading it is only an **analogy** between holographic entropy and
cosmic structures. It does **not** claim structure sources dark energy. Not a match.

### 4. Timescape cosmology (Wiltshire)
Genuinely "structure → acceleration": as bound structures decouple from expansion, gravitational-
energy / clock-rate differences produce acceleration; a 2024 analysis claims supernova evidence over
ΛCDM.
- **Difference:** timescape says there is **no real dark energy** — acceleration is an inhomogeneity/
  averaging *artifact*. SEDE asserts dark energy is **real**, just structure-fed.

### 5. Cosmologically coupled black holes (Farrah, Croker et al., 2023)
A specific kind of structure — **black holes** — sources a real DE-like term (measured coupling ≈ 3,
zero ruled out at 99.98%).
- **Difference:** the mechanism is black-hole **vacuum-energy interiors growing with expansion**, not
  horizon-entropy deposition from *all* bound structure. Also **actively contested** (published
  rebuttals; a 2025 paper rules out a related variant against DESI/Union3).

### 6. Matter creation / CCDM (Lima, Prigogine lineage)
Gravitationally-induced particle creation gives a "creation pressure" p_c = −Γρ/3H (negative) that
drives acceleration without dark energy; non-equilibrium thermodynamics with entropy production. Active
DESI-era revival ("Is dark energy non-equilibrium thermodynamics?", 2025).
- **Difference:** the source is **matter creation** (energy from the gravitational field → new
  particles), not the entropy of *existing* structure; and it typically replaces DE rather than being a
  real DE fed by structure.

### 7. Padmanabhan holographic equipartition (dV/dt = N_sur − N_bulk)
Cosmic expansion driven by the surface-vs-bulk degree-of-freedom difference; "expansion demands dark
energy" to reach equipartition.
- **Difference:** information-theoretic counting of horizon dof, not structure-sourced; gives the area
  law / standard cosmology, the baseline SEDE departs from.

### 8. Configuration / gravitational entropy of structure
Configuration entropy as a thermodynamic probe of clustering, sometimes correlated with late-time
acceleration and entropy flow.
- **Difference:** entropy is used as a *diagnostic* of structure, not as the *source* of a real DE term.

## The distinguishing line (revised)
With Gough's HDIE/IDE in hand, the honest statement is narrower than before:
- **The idea "structure's entropy *is* dark energy" is NOT SEDE's — it is Gough's (2011, with 2025
  evidence).** Both share the headline, the self-regulation, the coincidence solution, and a structure-
  history-driven DE fit.
- **What may still be SEDE-specific is the *mechanism*:** real dark energy from the **gravitational
  binding entropy of bound structure deposited on the apparent horizon** (Clausius, ρ_DE = T_AH·s_grav),
  with a **Barrow volume-law (Δ=1)** entropy, a **parameter-free** dark sector, and **w crossing −1** —
  versus Gough's **Landauer information energy of star-heated baryons**. These are physically different
  sources (gravitational/horizon entropy vs thermal/information entropy of hot gas).
- Among the *other* families: entropic-force/Barrow/Tsallis source DE from the **horizon** (structure
  downstream); timescape makes acceleration **apparent**; coupled BHs use **one structure**; CCDM uses
  **matter creation**; Padmanabhan/configuration-entropy are **diagnostics/baselines**.

**Revised one-liner:** SEDE is **not** the first to say structure feeds dark energy (Gough is) — it is a
**horizon-thermodynamic / Barrow-volume-law realization** of that idea, and *that specific mechanism* is
the only part with a remaining novelty claim, pending a formal priority pass.

## INSPIRE-HEP systematic pass (June 2026, open API, citation-ranked)
Queried the open INSPIRE REST API across several free-text phrasings (fielded `abstract:` syntax
returned spurious zeros and was abandoned; free-text works). Key results:

- **"information dark energy" OR "dark information energy" → 6 papers, ALL by M. P. Gough** (2011
  HDIE, 2012, 2013, 2016, **2022 "Information Dark Energy Can Resolve the Hubble Tension"**, 2025
  star-formation evidence). **No other author** proposes information/entropy-of-structure dark energy
  in INSPIRE. So the structure-sourced-DE *idea* is not only Gough's by priority — he is **essentially
  its sole proponent**, and it is **under-cited** (max 10 citations). The idea exists but never caught
  on — an under-explored lane, not a crowded one.
- **"binding energy" + "dark energy" + "apparent horizon" → 0 papers.** SEDE's *specific mechanism*
  (gravitational binding entropy of structure deposited on the apparent horizon) has **no INSPIRE
  match** — supporting mechanism-level distinctiveness (consistent with the general-web result).
- **"structure-sourced"/"structure-gated dark energy" → 0.** SEDE's terminology is unused (no
  naming collision).
- The general entropy→DE cluster in INSPIRE is the **entropic-force / entropic-cosmology** family
  ("Entropic-force dark energy reconsidered" 80 cites; "Constraining Entropic Cosmology" 37 cites) —
  horizon-sourced, already catalogued; structure appears only as a downstream consequence.

**Scope caveat (important):** INSPIRE is **HEP/theory-weighted**. It indexed Gough only thinly and did
**not** surface the broader *astro* entropic-cosmology literature (Komatsu, Lima/CCDM, the 2024–26
"mass-to-horizon entropy" wave) that the general-web pass found — those live in astro journals better
covered by **NASA ADS**. So the INSPIRE pass is conclusive on HEP/theory but **must be complemented by
an ADS pass** (needs a free API token, or run by hand) for full observational-cosmology coverage.

**Net (after INSPIRE):** the structure-sourced-DE *idea* = Gough's, and remarkably isolated; SEDE's
*horizon-binding-entropy mechanism* and *terminology* show no precedent in INSPIRE.

## NASA ADS systematic pass (June 2026, authenticated API, citation-ranked) — COMPLETES the check
Ran 8 Solr queries over ADS (astro coverage INSPIRE lacks). Results:

- **SEDE's mechanism** `abs:("dark energy" "binding energy" horizon)` → **2 hits, both irrelevant**
  (a photon Bose-condensate paper; a muonium experiment). With INSPIRE's 0, the gravitational-
  binding-entropy-on-the-apparent-horizon mechanism has **no precedent in either database.**
- **Exact terminology** (`"structure-sourced/structure-gated dark energy"`) → **0**; `holographic DE +
  structure growth source` → **0**. No collision.
- **Gough's priority is DEEPER than first found — back to 2006–2008:** "On the Similarity of
  Information Energy to Dark Energy" (2006), **"Information Equation of State" (2008, 16 cit — his
  most-cited)**, "The Accelerating Universe and the Second Law" (2009), then HDIE (2011) → IDE (2025).
  A ~20-year, still-under-cited (≤16), essentially **solo** lineage. So credit Gough from **2006/2008**,
  not just 2011.
- **No other author** proposing structure/star-formation-sourced *entropic/information* DE surfaced.
  The generalised-entropy / Barrow / Kaniadakis / fractal-BH cosmology hits (2024–26) are
  horizon-sourced with structure downstream — already catalogued.
- Confirms the entropic-force family (Basilakos "Entropic-force DE reconsidered" 79 cit; Mathew 2016)
  as horizon-sourced, not structure-sourced.

**FINAL VERDICT (INSPIRE + ADS both done):** (1) the *idea* "structure's entropy/information ≈ dark
energy" is **Gough's, since ~2006–2008**, isolated and under-explored; SEDE must cite him as priority.
(2) SEDE's *specific mechanism* (gravitational binding entropy of bound structure on the apparent
horizon, ρ_DE=T_AH·s_grav, Barrow Δ=1, gravity-driven) and its *terminology* have **no precedent in
either INSPIRE or ADS** — this is the defensible novelty. (3) The apples-to-apples CAMB MCMC
(run_gough_mcmc.py) shows both are viable, SEDE modestly best (ΔDIC −3.3 vs Gough −0.45). The priority
pass is now reasonably thorough (not provably exhaustive, but both authoritative databases swept).
