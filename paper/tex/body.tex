\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
The standard model $\LCDM$ fits an extraordinary range of data with a cosmological constant $\Lambda$
whose value confronts two long-standing puzzles. The \emph{cosmological-constant problem}: if $\Lambda$
is the energy of the quantum vacuum, na\"ive effective field theory overshoots the observed value by
$\sim10^{120}$. The \emph{coincidence problem}: $\rhoDE\sim\rho_{\mathrm m}$ today despite scaling
differently with expansion. Recent DESI~DR2 results~\cite{DESI:2025zgx} give a mild preference for
\emph{evolving} dark energy ($w$ crossing $-1$), reopening whether $\Lambda$ is fundamental.

We approach this through an accounting question. As structure forms, collapsing halos release
gravitational binding energy, and dark energy activates over the \emph{same} epoch that structure
formation saturates. A one-line budget rules out an energy supply: a virialised system has
$|E_{\rm bind}|/Mc^2\sim\sigma^2/c^2\sim10^{-5}$ (clusters; galaxies $\sim10^{-7}$), so the
binding-energy density is $\sim10^{6}$ times too small to \emph{be} $\rhoDE$. The link, if any, cannot
be energetic.

The resolution is thermodynamic: we identify dark energy with the conjugate thermal energy density of
the cosmic horizon (motivated by horizon thermodynamics~\cite{Jacobson:1995ab,Cai:2005ra}),
\begin{equation}
\rhoDE=\TAH\,\sgrav\,\fsat(z),
\label{eq:ansatz}
\end{equation}
whose \emph{scale} is the horizon's own, $\TAH s\sim\Mpl^2H^2\sim\rhocr$, rather than the QFT vacuum
(so the vacuum-energy-scale problem is recast, not inherited). What structure supplies is not energy
but entropy bookkeeping: by the horizon first law $\delta Q=T\,dS$, halo virialisation fixes the
\emph{redshift dependence} of the activation fraction $\fsat(z)\in[0,1]$, while its amplitude and the
$\fsat(0)=1$ normalisation are set by the horizon-scale normalisation. The coincidence is then a
consequence of the mechanism, not a tuning.

The idea that dark energy is tied to the entropy/information produced as structure forms originates
with Gough's information dark energy~\cite{Gough2008,Gough:2011eq,Gough2025}, whose priority we credit;
what is specific to SEDE is the \emph{mechanism} (a gravitational horizon-entropy realisation, driven
by the gravitational growth factor rather than the baryonic star-formation history) and a
parameter-free dark sector, quantified in Sec.~\ref{sec:relations}.

By \emph{$\Lambda$-free} we mean the late-time acceleration is not a constant stress-energy term but a
self-conserved horizon fluid whose present amplitude is fixed by flatness
($\ODEz=1-\Om-\Orad$)---exactly as flat $\LCDM$ fixes $\Omega_{\Lambda 0}$---and whose redshift
dependence is set by the horizon/growth prescription. The distinctive feature is that the dark sector
adds \emph{no fitted parameter} (Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}); the price is a single postulate about the
quantum-gravitational structure of the horizon (Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}).

\section{The model and energy conservation}\label{sec:model}

\paragraph{Conjugate horizon-fluid ansatz.}
At the flat-FRW apparent horizon $\RAH=1/H$, the Cai--Kim temperature is
$\TAH=(H/2\pi)(1-\varepsilon/2)$, $\varepsilon\equiv-\dot H/H^2$; the $O(1)$ factor is absorbed into
the $\fsat(0)=1$ normalisation, so the headline model uses $\TAH=H/2\pi$. We take $\sgrav$ to be the
coarse-grained entanglement entropy in the apparent-horizon \emph{volume},
$S_{\rm grav}\propto\VAH\propto\RAH^3$---a \emph{constant} entropy density $\sgrav=s_0$ (in contrast to
the Bekenstein--Hawking area-law density $s_{\rm AH}\propto H$). With $\TAH\propto H$,
\begin{equation}
\rhoDE=\TAH\,s_0\,\fsat\;\propto\;H\,\fsat,\qquad
s_0=\frac{\ODEz\,\rho_{\rm crit,0}}{T_{\mathrm{AH},0}}
\label{eq:rhoH}
\end{equation}
(the constant $s_0$ fixed by flatness),
i.e.\ $\rhoDE\propto H\fsat$ with no free parameter. This volume law is the maximal Barrow deformation
($S\propto A^{1+\Delta/2}=A^{3/2}=R^3$ at $\Delta=1$, $\lambda=1-\Delta/2=1/2$); $\Delta$ is retained
only as a \emph{test} parameter (Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}).

\paragraph{Structure gate.}
Structure formation supplies the redshift dependence of the gate through the collapsed-halo abundance,
giving the closed form
\begin{equation}
\fsat(z)=\frac{1-e^{-\gamma D^2(z)}}{1-e^{-\gamma}},
\label{eq:fsat}
\end{equation}
with $D(z)$ the model's self-consistent linear growth factor (computed with SEDE's own $E(z)$), the
normalisation $\fsat(0)=1$, and $\fsat\to0$ at high $z$. The single shape parameter $\gamma$ is fixed
by a halo-entropy prescription (Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}), not fitted.

\paragraph{Friedmann fixed point.}
Inserting $\rhoDE$ into the unmodified Friedmann constraint gives a single fixed-point equation for
$E=H/H_0$,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\,E^2(z)=\Om(1+z)^3+\Orad(1+z)^4+\ODEz\,\fsat(z)\,E(z)\,}
\label{eq:friedmann}
\end{equation}
(for $\lambda=1/2$, so $E^{2\lambda}=E$). Equation~\eqref{eq:friedmann} is the entire background model:
$\LCDM$ with the constant $\Lambda$ replaced by the structure-gated, horizon-coupled term
$\ODEz\fsat(z)E(z)$; at $z=0$ with $\fsat=1$ it reproduces $\ODEz=1-\Om$.

\paragraph{Replacement of $\Lambda$.}
SEDE is holographic dark energy, not modified gravity: the Einstein tensor and $G$ are untouched, and
the deformation enters only a smooth ($c_s^2=1$) perfect fluid,
\begin{equation}
\begin{gathered}
G_{\mu\nu}=8\pi G\,(T^{\rm (m)}_{\mu\nu}+T^{\rm (DE)}_{\mu\nu}),\\
\rhoDE=\tfrac{3}{8\pi G}\,\ODEz\,H H_0\,\fsat,\qquad \pDE=w(z)\rhoDE .
\end{gathered}
\label{eq:efe}
\end{equation}
Equivalently $\Lambda$ becomes a running scalar $\Lambda_{\rm eff}(z)=3\ODEz H H_0\fsat$. A
Barrow-\emph{modified-gravity} Friedmann equation would instead rescale the early-universe expansion by
$(\Mpl/H)^\Delta\sim10^{61}$, a nucleosynthesis catastrophe (Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}); keeping the
deformation in the fluid leaves the early universe exactly standard.

\paragraph{Energy conservation.}
Because SEDE is general relativity with a fluid source, the Bianchi identity forces total covariant
conservation; matter dilutes standardly and dark energy is a self-conserved fluid,
\begin{equation}
\dot\rhoDE+3H(1+w_{\rm DE})\rhoDE=0,\qquad
w_{\rm DE}(a)=-1-\tfrac13\frac{d\ln\rhoDE}{d\ln a}.
\label{eq:cons}
\end{equation}
There is no literal energy transfer from matter to dark energy: the magnitude of $\rhoDE$ is anchored
to the horizon scale $\sim\Mpl^2H^2\sim\rhocr$ through the Cohen--Kaplan--Nelson
bound~\cite{Cohen:1998zx} and flatness (Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}), while the binding energy plays a purely
entropic, shape-setting role.

\section{The fixed inputs: why the dark sector adds no fitted parameter}\label{sec:inputs}
In flat $\LCDM$ the dark-energy amplitude $\Omega_{\Lambda0}=1-\Om-\Orad$ is itself fixed by the
flatness closure, not an independent fit; SEDE keeps exactly this---no independent dark-energy amplitude
beyond flatness---and differs only in that the \emph{redshift dependence} is set by the horizon/growth
prescription rather than held constant. That prescription fixes the triplet
($\gamma$, $w(z)$-shape, $\lambda$) plus the sound speed $c_s^2$, and each is fixed by a stated
theoretical choice (not fitted to cosmological data), so SEDE adds no free dark-energy parameter. The
choices separate along the two scales of Sec.~\ref{sec:model}: the \emph{magnitude} of dark energy is
the horizon scale, $\sim\Mpl^2H^2\sim\rhocr$ (the CKN bound normalised by flatness,
Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}), while the choices control the \emph{shape}---\emph{when} and \emph{how sharply}
that energy turns on. We therefore lead with $\gamma$.

\subsection{The structure coupling $\gamma\approx1.5$ from a halo-binding-entropy prescription}
$\gamma$ sets the sharpness of the gate, $\gamma=d\ln\Sigma_S/d\ln\sigma_8$ with $\Sigma_S$ the
entropy-weighted collapsed mass, $\Sigma_S=\int M^p\,(dn/d\ln M)\,d\ln M$. The weight exponent $p$ is
fixed by \emph{where} the structure-formation entropy is deposited. Under the prescription that the
released gravitational binding energy $E_{\rm bind}\propto M^{5/3}$ is accounted in the
\emph{cosmic-horizon} entropy ledger (Sec.~\ref{sec:model}) at its temperature $\TAH\propto H$, the
Clausius relation adds $\Delta S_{\rm AH}=E_{\rm bind}/\TAH\propto M^{5/3}$ ($\TAH$ being
mass-independent at a fixed epoch). Hence $p=5/3$, and a standard Sheth--Tormen mass
function~\cite{Sheth:1999mn} gives $\gamma\approx1.50$, close to the value favoured in diagnostic
fits. This is a fixed prescription, not a universal constant: the numerical $\gamma$ depends on the
adopted mass function, mass and redshift range, and entropy-weighting choice, and is fixed
\emph{without fitting to cosmological data}. (The earlier expectation $p=1$ used the halo's internal
virial temperature $T_{\rm vir}\propto M^{2/3}$; the reservoir adopted here is the horizon, not the
halo.)

\subsection{The equation of state $w(z)$ from the background, conservation, and flatness}
The model has a single physical equation of state---that of the canonical fixed-point background,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:friedmann}---obtained from energy conservation, Eq.~\eqref{eq:cons}, with
$\rhoDE=\ODEz\rho_{\rm crit,0}E\fsat$ and the present-day normalisation fixed by spatial flatness
($\ODEz=1-\Om-\Orad$, $\fsat(0)=1$). Evaluated on the self-consistent background this gives
$w_0\approx-1.0$, \emph{crossing} $-1$ at low redshift ($z\approx0.2$) and dipping to $\approx-1.08$ by
$z\approx2$---DESI~DR2's thawing/crossing quadrant. So $w(z)$ is fixed by $\Om$ through the background
with no free EOS parameter; this is the $w(z)$ used throughout (Sec.~\ref{sec:results},
Fig.~\ref{fig:eos}). Three $w_0$ values appear in this paper for \emph{different} objects:

\subsection{The $H$-coupling $\lambda=1/2$ from the horizon's volume entropy}
$\lambda$ fixes the magnitude's scaling with $H$, with no free parameter: the gravitational entropy is
the coarse-grained entanglement entropy in the apparent-horizon \emph{volume}, $S_{\rm grav}\propto
\VAH\propto\RAH^3$, i.e.\ a constant horizon entropy density. The conjugate ansatz
($\rhoDE=\TAH\sgrav$, $\TAH\propto H$) then gives $\rhoDE\propto H\equiv H^{2\lambda}$ with
$\lambda=1/2$. Equivalently, in the Barrow language $S=(A/A_0)^{1+\Delta/2}$ this is the maximal
deformation $\Delta=1$ ($s_{\rm AH}\propto H^{1-\Delta}$, $\lambda=1-\Delta/2$): the volume law
\emph{is} $\Delta=1$ ($A^{3/2}=R^3=V$). We keep $\Delta$ only as a falsifiable test of the volume law
(Secs.~\ref{sec:results},~\ref{sec:pred}); Sec.~\ref{sec:qg} argues why the space-filling state is
selected.

\subsection{The sound speed $c_s^2=1$ from a background functional}
SEDE's dark energy is background-growth-gated, which might suggest it clusters. We adopt the minimal
smooth-dark-energy closure: $\fsat$ is a functional of the \emph{background} growth factor $D(z)$ (a
function of cosmic time only), so $\rhoDE$ is homogeneous by construction and
$\partial\fsat/\partial(\delta_{\rm m}^{\rm local})=0$, giving the standard metric-driven fluid
response with $c_s^2=1$. This is a modelling closure, not a theorem; a full CLASS Boltzmann treatment
confirms the resulting $f\sigma_8(z)$ matches this smooth-dark-energy growth to $0.1\%$ over
$z=0.3$--$1.5$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:data}).

\section{Data and methodology}\label{sec:data}

\subsection{Data}
We use a standard late-time-plus-compressed-CMB compilation, with both models fit to the identical data
vector: \emph{BAO}---DESI~DR2 (galaxy, QSO, and Ly$\alpha$ tracers, $z=0.3$--$2.33$) as $D_M/r_d$ and
$D_H/r_d$~\cite{DESI:2025zgx}; \emph{Supernovae}---Pantheon$+$ as the baseline, with DES-5YR and
Union3 used independently for the robustness cross-check of Sec.~\ref{sec:results};
\emph{Cosmic chronometers}---the Moresco~\textit{et al.} $H(z)$ compilation; \emph{Redshift-space
distortions}---the Gold-2018 $f\sigma_8(z)$ compilation (with the data audit of App.~\ref{app:data}
correcting a few mis-entered points); \emph{CMB}---the compressed Planck distance priors (shift
parameter $R$, acoustic scale $l_A$, $\omega_b$), with the full \texttt{plik\_lite} likelihood as a
cross-check; \emph{Local distance ladder}---the SH0ES $H_0$ prior; \emph{Out-of-sample}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:results})---the pre-DESI eBOSS~DR16 full-shape BAO, held entirely out of the main fit.

\subsection{CAMB-in-the-loop: a physical sound horizon}
The single most important methodological point is that the sound horizon is \emph{not} a free
parameter. Earlier exploratory fits that floated $r_d$ found large apparent preferences for SEDE; those
did not survive a physical sound horizon. Here, at every point in the chain, $r_{\rm drag}$,
$r_\star$, and $\theta_\star$ are computed self-consistently from the background by
CAMB~\cite{Lewis:1999bs} (``CAMB-in-the-loop''), so BAO and CMB distances are tied to the same
early-universe physics for both models. This makes the $\Delta\mathrm{DIC}$ of Sec.~\ref{sec:results} a
like-for-like comparison rather than an artifact of a free standard ruler. The CMB enters as the
compressed $(R,l_A)$ distances computed on the SEDE background through the same CAMB call. This
captures the background distance to last scattering (and hence the early-dark-energy fraction that
drives the $\Delta$ leverage of Sec.~\ref{sec:results}), but \emph{not} the full
acoustic-peak/polarisation or lensing/ISW information; a full Planck/ACT likelihood is the natural next
validation (Sec.~\ref{sec:disc}).

\subsection{Inference and model comparison}
Parameters are sampled by MCMC, marginalising over $\Om$, $H_0$ (with the SH0ES prior), $\omega_b$, the
amplitude/$\sigma_8$, and $r_d$-determining physics---the \emph{same five} for SEDE and $\LCDM$, since
SEDE's dark sector is fixed (Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}). Model comparison uses the deviance information
criterion (DIC), which penalises the effective number of parameters $p_D$; because $p_D$ is
numerically close for the two models, $\Delta\mathrm{DIC}$ reduces essentially to the $\chi^2$
difference. Significance is assessed not by $\Delta\mathrm{DIC}$ alone but by the false-preference
calibration of Sec.~\ref{sec:results}.

\subsection{Perturbations: smooth dark energy, validated by CLASS}
SEDE's dark energy has $c_s^2=1$ and does not cluster (Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}), so its growth is
captured analytically by a smooth-dark-energy treatment. We validated this against a full Boltzmann
computation: implemented in CLASS~\cite{Blas:2011rf} as a PPF fluid (which handles the $w=-1$
crossing), the full-perturbation $f\sigma_8(z)$ matches the analytic background-only growth to $0.1\%$
over $z=0.3$--$1.5$. The metric-level effects (CMB lensing $C_\ell^{\phi\phi}$, low-$\ell$ ISW,
$S_8$) are all at the percent level relative to $\LCDM$.
\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{The joint fit and the equation of state}
In the marginalised joint analysis, SEDE is mildly preferred over $\LCDM$, with a deviance information
criterion difference $\Delta\mathrm{DIC}\approx-2.9$ in SEDE's favour. Because the dark sector adds no
fitted parameter (Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}), the two models share the same five parameters and the same
effective complexity, $p_D(\mathrm{SEDE})\approx p_D(\LCDM)$: the preference is in fit, not bought with
parameters. The recovered background parameters are physical and close to $\LCDM$ ($\Om\approx0.30$,
$H_0\approx68.8$, $\sigma_8\approx0.76$). The absolute $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof}\approx3$
(the honest verdict below) reflects the heterogeneous, partly-compressed compilation and should
\emph{not} be read as a goodness-of-fit probability---we use only matched-model differences
($\Delta\mathrm{DIC}$, $\Delta\chi^2$) on the identical data vector. The canonical ($\lambda=1/2$)
equation of state \emph{crosses} $w=-1$, with a present-day value near $-1$ and a dip to $\approx-1.08$
by $z\approx2$, i.e.\ $(w_0,w_a)\approx(-0.98,-0.11)$---squarely in DESI~DR2's evolving-dark-energy
quadrant, $\sim2.7\sigma$ from the $\LCDM$ point. The crossing is not imposed; it is where the
expansion and horizon-entropy-production terms in $1+w$ balance (Sec.~\ref{sec:pred}).

\subsection{Is the preference real, or model flexibility?}
A $\Delta\mathrm{DIC}\approx-3$ is suggestive but not decisive, and a structure-gated model could in
principle win by flexibility rather than by capturing a real signal. We test this with a
\emph{false-preference calibration} (a parametric bootstrap). We generate mock datasets from a $\LCDM$
truth---re-drawing \emph{every} probe self-consistently, crucially including the compressed CMB
distances $(R,l_A)$ and the SH0ES prior, not just the late-time data---and refit both models to each
mock. Under $\LCDM$ truth a flexible model would still tend to ``win''; SEDE does not: the null
distribution of $\Delta\chi^2(\mathrm{SEDE}-\LCDM)$ has mean $+0.42$ (SEDE if anything fits
$\LCDM$-truth mocks slightly \emph{worse}, consistent with having no spare flexibility). The real data
give $\Delta\chi^2=-2.96$, beyond essentially all the null draws: in a 500-mock run,
$p\approx0.002$ ($\sim3\sigma$), one mock in 500.

We flag two honest caveats. First, an earlier version of this test held the CMB distances \emph{fixed}
across mocks; that biased the null mean to $-2.87$ and gave $p\approx0.5$---the artifact that made the
preference look like pure flexibility. Re-drawing the CMB self-consistently is the correct procedure
and yields $p\approx0.002$. Second, the significance is CMB-distance-treatment-dependent: an
independent sibling pipeline, with a different $l_A$/CAMB handling, obtains a milder real
$\Delta\chi^2\approx-1.98$ and $p\approx0.025$ ($\sim2\sigma$). Both analyses agree the preference is
not obviously attributable to model flexibility, and trace the $2\sigma$--$3\sigma$ spread to the
CMB-distance engine rather than to SH0ES. We therefore quote a suggestive $\sim2$--$3\sigma$, pending
independent reproduction.

\subsection{Out-of-sample check}
A further check against a flexibility explanation is out-of-sample prediction. We trained SEDE on
\emph{real pre-DESI data only} (eBOSS~DR16 full-shape BAO, LRG/QSO~\cite{eBOSS:2020yzd}) and used it to
predict the later, held-out DESI~DR2 BAO: in this split SEDE matches the held-out DESI~DR2 trend better
than $\LCDM$ by $\Delta\chi^2=-8.2$; a low-$z\to$high-$z$ split gives $-9.9$. A model winning purely by
flexibility would tend to do \emph{worse} out of sample, so a pre-DESI-trained SEDE realisation
predicting the DESI~DR2 trend is encouraging. The result should be independently reproduced before
strong weight is placed on it.

\subsection{Robustness}
The preference is not a single-dataset artifact. (i)~\emph{Supernova-set independence:}
$\Delta\chi^2(\mathrm{SEDE}-\LCDM)$ is $-2.95/-2.87/-2.97$ with Pantheon$+$/DES-5YR/Union3
respectively. (ii)~\emph{Leave-one-probe-out:} dropping any one probe leaves $\Delta\chi^2$ negative in
every case (range $[-3.06,-2.16]$, mean $-2.64$)---a multi-probe consensus. (iii)~\emph{Code
independence:} the SEDE expansion history reproduced with CLASS matches CAMB to $5\times10^{-5}$ in
$r_{\rm drag}$ ($\ll0.2\%$). (iv)~\emph{Growth:} the full-Boltzmann $\gamma_{\rm growth}\approx0.55$
confirms standard-GR dark energy, not modified gravity. (v)~\emph{Tracer-level robustness:} recent
analyses argue the DESI~DR2 evolving-DE signal is driven mainly by the LRG1 ($z\approx0.51$) and LRG2
($z\approx0.71$) BAO bins. Dropping these bins individually and together leaves SEDE's preference
intact---$\Delta\chi^2=-3.27$ ($-$LRG1), $-2.76$ ($-$LRG2), and $-3.00$ with both removed (versus
$-2.95$ for the full set). SEDE's mild preference is therefore \emph{not} the LRG1--2 artefact the
steep-CPL signal is attributed to: the $\LCDM$-deviation measured by the early-vs-late
$\mathrm{ratio}(\omega_m)$ metric drops from $2.6\sigma$ to $1.2\sigma$ when LRG1--2 are removed,
whereas SEDE's $\Delta\chi^2$ is essentially unchanged---SEDE's preference and the LRG-driven steep-CPL
deviation are \emph{different objects}.

\subsection{Honest verdict}

motivating the volume endpoint:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Geometric ceiling.} The horizon's fractal dimension is $d_H=2+\Delta$, capped at the
space-filling value $d_H=3$ for a two-surface in three-space. Since $\Delta=2/(d-1)$, the case $d=3$
gives $\Delta=1$: a clean endpoint, not an awkward intermediate.
\item \emph{Thermodynamic floor / GSL.} The horizon entropy is monotone toward the volume endpoint, and
because $\rhoDE=T\!\cdot\!s$ makes the free energy $F=E-TS$ vanish identically, entropy itself is the
governing potential; the generalised second law selects the volume-law maximum. Volume-law scaling is
the signature of a thermalised, maximally-scrambled state.
\item \emph{The holographic bound fixes the scale, not the form.} The CKN bound pins the magnitude but
not the exponent---its own state count gives a sub-area-law. The volume law is a statement about the
horizon's quantum \emph{state} (typical/thermalised), which the energy bound cannot supply.
\item \emph{A driven steady state.} The horizon need not self-thermalise---its scrambling time
($\sim\ln S/H\approx280/H$) far exceeds its age. The structure-growth gate drives it off the ground
state; with a scrambling-limited relaxation rate, the volume-law state is maintained as a
non-equilibrium steady state---a fixed point of SEDE's own dynamics.
\item \emph{A roughening universality class} (developed with the sibling collaboration). Modelling the
horizon as a stochastic growing surface, the Edwards--Wilkinson (linear) class flows to the volume
endpoint and corresponds to SEDE's purely-entropic $F=0$ condition. The most speculative route,
included only as motivation.
\item \emph{An independent emergent-gravity proposal.} In Verlinde's programme~\cite{Verlinde:2016toy}
the de Sitter vacuum carries a volume-law contribution to its entanglement entropy---precisely the
Barrow endpoint of our family. A second, independently-motivated route to a volume-law entropy makes
the postulate less \emph{ad hoc}.
\end{enumerate}
Finally, the area-law branch is disfavoured directly: saturating the CKN bound is algebraically the
area-law case $\Delta=0$, whose EOS ($w_0=-0.49$) and large early-dark-energy fraction are disfavoured
in the conditional diagnostics of Sec.~\ref{sec:results}.

\subsection{Why $\rhoDE\sim\rhocr$: the CKN bound and the vacuum-energy scale}
The deformation fixes the scaling with $H$ but not the magnitude. The magnitude comes from the
Cohen--Kaplan--Nelson holographic energy bound~\cite{Cohen:1998zx}: an effective field theory in a
region of size $L$ cannot hold more energy than a black hole of that size, $\rho L^3\lesssim\Mpl^2 L$.
With the IR cutoff at the cosmic horizon, $L=c/H$,
\begin{equation}
\rhoDE\lesssim\Mpl^2H^2\sim\rhocr\quad\Rightarrow\quad\Omega_{\rm DE}\sim\mathcal{O}(1).
\label{eq:ckn}
\end{equation}
The naïve QFT vacuum, $\rho\sim\Mpl^4$, overshoots this by $\rho_P/\rhocr\sim10^{122}$---the
cosmological-constant problem. SEDE never invokes the QFT vacuum: dark energy is the horizon's conjugate
thermal energy density, and CKN supplies the horizon-scale upper bound. This is a bound, not an equality:
the present amplitude is fixed by spatial flatness ($\ODEz=1-\Om$), in the same bookkeeping sense that
$\LCDM$ fixes $\Omega_{\Lambda0}$. The same bound, read as a UV--IR relation, gives a gravitating-vacuum
cutoff $\Lambda_{\rm UV}=(\Mpl H_0)^{1/2}\approx4$\,meV---numerically the observed dark-energy scale.

\subsection{The seam, BBN-safety, and the holographic scope}
A naïve microscopic volume-law entropy density would be Planckian, so a naïve $\rhoDE=T\!\cdot\!(S_{\rm
vol}/V)$ would overshoot $\rhocr$ by $(\Mpl/H)^\Delta\sim10^{61}$---the square root of the $10^{122}$
hierarchy. The same factor controls three apparently separate issues---this seam, the BBN bound on a
Barrow-modified Friedmann equation, and the modified-gravity-vs-holographic fork---which are one object.
The resolution is the holographic-DE scope of Sec.~\ref{sec:model}: the deformation acts only on the
dark-energy fluid (magnitude from CKN, scaling from Barrow), not on the gravitational sector. The
early-universe expansion is then exactly standard ($\Delta N_{\rm eff}=\Delta Y_p=0$; BBN-safe), and the
naïve $T\!\cdot\!(S/V)$ is precisely the modified-gravity path the scope forbids. This is the
load-bearing assumption of the model. In the modified-gravity reading, BBN forces
$\Delta\lesssim1.4\times10^{-4}$~\cite{Barrow:2020tzx}, which read literally would exclude SEDE's
$\Delta=1$. Those bounds do \emph{not} apply: SEDE keeps standard gravity and lets the volume-law
entropy act only on the dark-energy fluid, so there is no extra Friedmann term and $\Delta$ is
unconstrained by them. The headline $\Delta=1$ rests on this single scope choice---legitimate and
standard (it \emph{is} the holographic-dark-energy framework), but not free.

\textbf{Postulate (volume-law horizon).} The cosmic apparent horizon carries the coarse-grained

\end{quote}
The results above are bounds, consistency arguments, or motivations \emph{given} this postulate; the

fitted deformation parameter: $\Delta$ is the falsifiable test of this postulate. We do not claim to
have derived quantum gravity; we claim to have reduced its dark-energy content to one testable statement
and removed it as a fitted parameter. A volume-law \emph{entanglement} entropy is at least not exotic in
cosmological field theory: it grows and saturates after a de Sitter$\to$flat

\section{Discussion: open problems and honest assessment}\label{sec:disc}

\paragraph{The one open item.} Every dark-sector input is fixed by a stated choice
(Sec.~\ref{sec:inputs}) given the single postulate that the horizon is volume-law-entangled
(Sec.~\ref{sec:qg}). That postulate is motivated by gravity's non-extensivity and the second law but is
not derived from a microscopic state count; it is the model's one irreducible input, and we have argued
it is not closable by reframing (canonical vs microcanonical accounting gives either area-law
$\lambda=1$ or Planckian-volume $\lambda=1/2$, never both for free)---a genuine, and falsifiable, open
problem.

\paragraph{Scope and tensions.} $H_0$ is out of SEDE's scope (it is $r_d$-pinned). The
structure-sourcing \emph{mechanism}---the defining claim that $\fsat$ tracks growth---is only weakly
confirmed: a geometry-vs-structure consistency test tracks at $z<1$ but is RSD-limited at $z>1$ and
remains inconclusive pending Euclid/LSST weak-lensing tomography. We separated this mechanism into a
derived, parameter-free part (the growth--expansion lock P2, Sec.~\ref{sec:pred}) and an optional
near-critical layer (the kill test P4); both are testable with that data. The honest framing: SEDE's
phenomenology ($w$ crossing $-1$, the volume-law endpoint) is mildly favoured with an encouraging
out-of-sample check, but its distinctive mechanism is the unconfirmed frontier.

\paragraph{A red-team of the recent literature.} We list the strongest recent results that could damage
SEDE.
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Holographic DE disfavoured by full CMB.} Wu \textit{et al.}~\cite{Wu:2025vfs} find
holographic and Ricci DE disfavoured by full ACT~DR6$+$DESI through an early-vs-late tension that
compressed $(R,l_A)$ priors hide. We tested it: against the full Planck \texttt{plik\_lite}
($\theta_\star$-matched) SEDE's $w(z)$ gives $\Delta\chi^2_{\rm CMB}=-0.50$ vs $\LCDM$, and against the
official ACT~DR6 CMB-lensing likelihood $\Delta\chi^2_{\rm ACT\text{-}lens}=-0.32$. Both
$|\Delta\chi^2|<1$: SEDE does not inherit the standard-HDE tension, because it has \emph{less} early
dark energy than $\LCDM$ ($\Omega_{\rm DE}(z{=}3)\approx0.035<0.040$), not the runaway high-$z$
behaviour that breaks original HDE.
\item \emph{Cosmic birefringence.} SEDE predicts $\beta=0$; a nonzero rotation is reported (ACT~DR6
$2.9\sigma$~\cite{Eskilt:2022cff}). \emph{If} cosmic and dark-energy-sourced, a single axion explains
$\beta+$acceleration---a parsimony threat. But the cosmic nature is not established (the measurement is
$\alpha+\beta$, Planck and ACT disagree by ${>}3\sigma$, the $\beta\times$LSS correlation is null at
SEDE's predicted zero). A contingent threat, not a present falsification; $\beta=0$ is symmetry-derived
and tied to GW-safety, so a confirmed cosmic $\beta$ would also predict GW parity violation.
\item \emph{The DDE signal as a low-$z$ SN systematic.} Huang, Cai \& Wang~\cite{Huang:2025som} show the
DESI preference is biased by an external low-$z$ SN sample in DESY5 and drops to ${<}2\sigma$ once
corrected. The bias is DESY5-specific; SEDE's headline uses Pantheon$+$, and its leave-one-probe-out
drops supernovae entirely and stays preferred ($\Delta\chi^2=-2.47$). SEDE is not SN-driven---but if the
field's evolving-DE evidence softens, SEDE's context erodes.
\item \emph{The nearest Barrow-HDE fit prefers $\Delta\ll1$.} Luciano, Paliathanasis \&
Saridakis~\cite{Luciano:2025elo} fit standard Barrow-HDE to DESI~DR2$+$SN$+$CC and obtain
$\Delta<0.47$--$0.54$, which read literally disfavours $\Delta=1$. But their $\Delta$ enters the HDE
density directly, whereas SEDE's enters via $\lambda=1-\Delta/2$ \emph{and} the structure gate, which
decouples $\Delta$ from the EOS amplitude; in SEDE's own construction $\Delta=1$ is data-preferred over
$\Delta=0$. The bound constrains a sibling model, not SEDE.
\end{enumerate}
Net: the two threats targeting the model itself (full-CMB tension; the Barrow-$\Delta$ bound) are
answered by direct tests or a stated model distinction; the two targeting the context (birefringence;
the low-$z$ SN systematic) are real and partially outside SEDE's control.

\paragraph{Evidential status.} SEDE is a falsifiable, fixed-parameter, $\Lambda$-free alternative to
$\LCDM$, mildly preferred (suggestive $\sim2$--$3\sigma$) with an encouraging out-of-sample check,
resting on one open postulate and a few stated theoretical choices. It is stronger than
``indistinguishable from $\LCDM$'' and weaker than ``established.'' Its value is twofold: a physical
story (no fine-tuned $\Lambda$, $w(z)$ from $\Om$, the coincidence reframed) and a decidable prediction
(the volume law, $\Delta=1$). The data, not the theory, will settle it.

\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:concl}
We have presented Structural Entropy Dark Energy: a fixed-parameter dark-energy model in which dark
energy is identified with the conjugate thermal energy density of a volume-law-entangled cosmic horizon
and activated through a structure-growth gate. The model is a single Friedmann fixed-point equation with
no fitted dark-sector parameter---$\lambda$, $w(z)$, $\gamma$, and $c_s^2$ are each fixed by a stated
theoretical choice rather than tuned---that replaces the cosmological constant by a structure-gated,
horizon-coupled term. It addresses the vacuum-energy-scale problem (the scale is the CKN holographic
bound, normalised by flatness, not the QFT vacuum) and reframes the coincidence problem (the activation
gate tracks the late-time maturation of structure growth). In a marginalised, CAMB-in-the-loop joint
analysis it is mildly preferred over $\LCDM$ ($\Delta\mathrm{DIC}\approx-2.9$); the preference is
suggestive (calibration $\sim2$--$3\sigma$, not obviously flexibility) with an encouraging
pre-DESI~$\to$~DESI out-of-sample check, and its equation of state crosses $-1$ in DESI's evolving-DE
quadrant. Its quantum-gravitational content reduces to one falsifiable postulate---a volume-law horizon
entropy. The sharp future test is near: under the stated Fisher assumptions, DESI~DR3 and Euclid could
constrain the effective deformation $\Delta$ to $\sigma\approx0.09$, separating SEDE's volume-law
horizon from a smooth one at $\sim11\sigma$. SEDE is suggestive, not established---and, crucially, it is
decidable.

\subsection{The conjugate horizon-fluid ansatz}
SEDE \emph{identifies} the dark-energy density with the conjugate energy of the horizon entropy,
$\rhoDE=\TAH\sgrav\fsat$. The Clausius relation supplies the dynamics; the absolute density $\rho=Ts$ is
the model's defining \emph{ansatz}. At the flat-FRW apparent horizon $\RAH=1/H$, Hayward's first law
$\delta E=\TAH dS_{\rm AH}+W\delta V$ applied to a shell crossing the horizon gives the Clausius
relation $(\rho+p)HV=\TAH\dot S_{\rm AH}$ (standard, dynamical). The covariant entropy
bound~\cite{Bousso:2002ju} $S_{\rm matter}\le A/(4l_P^2)=S_{\rm AH}$ motivates a bounded $\fsat\in[0,1]$.
SEDE then posits
\begin{equation}
\rhoDE\equiv\TAH s_0\fsat=\frac{H}{2\pi}s_0\fsat\propto H\fsat,
\end{equation}
and fixing $s_0$ by flatness [$\rhoDE(0)=\ODEz\rho_{\rm crit,0}$, $\fsat(0)=1$] gives
$\rhoDE(z)=\ODEz\rho_{\rm crit,0}E(z)\fsat(z)$---the volume-law ($\lambda=1/2$) scaling of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:friedmann}, not the area-law $H^2$.

\subsection{The structure gate $\fsat(z)$}
Modelling the activation gate through a normalised growth-weighted deposition kernel $K\propto
(d\sigma_8^2/dt)e^{-\gamma\sigma_8^2/\sigma_8^2(0)}$ and taking $\fsat$ as its normalised cumulative,
the Hubble factors cancel and with $u\equiv\sigma_8^2/\sigma_8^2(0)$ running to $x(z)=D^2(z)$,
\begin{equation}
\fsat(z)=\frac{\int_0^{x(z)}e^{-\gamma u}du}{\int_0^1 e^{-\gamma u}du}
=\frac{1-e^{-\gamma x(z)}}{1-e^{-\gamma}},\quad x(z)=D^2(z),
\end{equation}
the CDF of a truncated exponential of rate $\gamma$ on $x\in[0,1]$, with $\fsat(0)=1$ and
$\fsat(z\gg1)\to0$.

\subsection{The fixed structure coupling $\gamma\approx1.496$}
In the horizon-fluid ansatz, $\fsat$ is the activated fraction of the cosmic-horizon entropy capacity,
so the entropy that defines its weighting is accounted in the horizon reservoir at $\TAH=H/2\pi$. When a
halo of mass $M$ virialises it releases $E_{\rm bind}\propto M^{5/3}$; by Clausius $\Delta S_{\rm
AH}=E_{\rm bind}/\TAH\propto M^{5/3}/H$. Because $\TAH\propto H$ is mass-independent at a fixed epoch,
the per-halo weight is $M^{5/3}$, i.e.\ $p=5/3$. With $\Sigma_S=\int M^{5/3}(dn/d\ln M)d\ln M$ and a
Sheth--Tormen mass function~\cite{Sheth:1999mn},
$\gamma=d\ln\Sigma_S^{(5/3)}/d\ln\sigma_8=1.4964\approx1.50$.

\subsection{The structural-EOS diagnostic (the $w_0$ side-note)}

struct}(0)=-1+\tfrac13 df_{\rm sat}/dx|_{x=1}$, and
\begin{equation}
\left.\frac{df_{\rm sat}}{dx}\right|_{x=1}=\frac{\gamma e^{-\gamma}}{1-e^{-\gamma}}=\frac{\gamma}{e^\gamma-1},
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\rhoDE=\TAH s_\Delta\fsat\propto H^{2-\Delta}\fsat\equiv H^{2\lambda}\fsat,\quad\lambda=1-\tfrac{\Delta}{2},
\end{equation}

postulate). The Barrow exponent multiplies only the $\fsat$-gated $\rhoDE$, not the gravitational
sector, so $H(z)$ at BBN is standard and the modified-gravity bound $\Delta\lesssim10^{-4}$ does not
apply.
